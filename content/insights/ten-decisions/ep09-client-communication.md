## POST

<!-- gate:body -->

Clients don't file grievances because they lost. They file because they couldn't reach anyone for 5 months and concluded nothing was happening.

Poor communication is one of the most common categories of bar grievance, and it's the one that has nothing to do with legal skill. A firm can handle a file well and still generate the complaint, because the client is grading a process they can't see.

That's the actual problem. The client has no visibility into your file. Records take 6 weeks, liens take months, and from the outside all of it looks identical to nothing.

Silence during a slow phase isn't neutral. The client fills it, and they fill it with the worst available explanation.

The reflex fix is telling everyone to communicate more, which produces uneven effort and no coverage. What works is a contact schedule that fires whether or not there's news, because "no news, here's what we're waiting on and how long it usually takes" is itself the reassurance.

> **Every client hears from the firm on this schedule, whether or not anything has happened:**
>
> | Stage | Cadence | Who |
> |---|---|---|
> | Intake to treatment | Every 30 days | Paralegal |
> | Treatment to demand | Every 30 days, plus the 21-day treatment-gap call from decision 4 | Paralegal |
> | Demand pending | Every 21 days | Paralegal |
> | Negotiation | Every offer, within 1 business day, always | **Attorney** |
> | Litigation | Every 30 days, plus every court date and deposition | Paralegal, attorney for strategy |
> | Settlement to disbursement | Every 14 days until the check clears | Paralegal |
>
> **Attorney-only, no exceptions:** any offer, any recommendation to accept or reject, any change in case value, any bad development, any legal advice. Staff never delivers a number the client has to react to.
>
> **The scripted line for a no-news contact:** what we're waiting on, who has it, how long it usually takes, and the date of the next update. 4 elements, 3 minutes, and it ends the "nothing is happening" story before it starts.
>
> **Everything gets logged.** An unlogged call didn't happen when the grievance arrives, and the log is the entire defense.
>
> **Exception:** a client who asks to be contacted less gets that in writing, in the file, and still receives the attorney contacts.

The disbursement row is the one firms skip and it's where the anger concentrates, because the client already knows the number by then and every day of silence is a day they think you're sitting on their money.

That's decision 9 of 10.
