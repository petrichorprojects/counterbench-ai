## Steps

1. Lead with the simplest liability story (what a juror would understand).
2. Present damages as a table plus narrative.
3. Address the top defense themes with concise rebuttals.
4. State negotiation posture (opening, target, floor) and why.

## Copy/Paste Prompt

```text
Draft a plaintiff-side mediation brief.

Inputs:
- Venue/jurisdiction: [ ]
- Liability story: [PASTE]
- Key exhibits: [LIST]
- Injuries/treatment: [PASTE]
- Economic damages: [PASTE]
- Non-economic impacts: [PASTE]
- Defense themes: [PASTE]
- Settlement posture: opening/target/floor + rationale

Output:
- Mediation brief (2–4 pages)
- Include: Liability, Damages, Defense rebuttals, Settlement posture
- Tone: credible, concise, mediator-friendly
- Do not over-argue; show proof.
```

## Quality Checks
- Numbers are consistent across sections.
- Rebuttals address the strongest defense theme first.
- Ends with a clear ask.
