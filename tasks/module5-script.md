# Module 5: "What Happens After Today" — Final 15-Minute Script

**Context**: This is the closing section of Module 5, added after the existing action plan + buddy system + community access content. Total Module 5 time increases from 30 min to 45 min. This section runs last — attendees have already identified their 3 tasks and paired up with accountability buddies.

**Purpose**: Transition from "you learned something" to "your firm has a structural problem." Opens VerdictOps pipeline through a free AI Readiness Assessment offer.

**Tone**: Honest, direct, peer-to-peer. Not a pitch — a reality check from someone who's seen 50 firms try this.

---

## PART 1: The Capacity Reality Check (5 min)

### [Slide: "The Honest Part"]

**Script:**

> Alright — you've got your three tasks. You've got your buddy. You've got the community. That's the plan, and it works.
>
> But before we wrap, I want to give you the honest part. Because I've run enough of these to know what happens next.

**[Pause. Let the room settle.]**

> Here's the pattern I see. You go back to your firm Monday morning. You're fired up. You try the first prompt. It works. You save 20 minutes on something that used to take an hour. You think — this is real.
>
> Then Tuesday happens. The discovery pile that was there Friday is still there. Your inbox has 47 new emails. A partner drops an urgent motion on your desk. And the AI thing you were going to try? It slides.
>
> By Friday, you're back to how things were. Not because you forgot. Not because AI doesn't work. Because the workload didn't change.

**[Look around the room.]**

> Raise your hand if your firm is understaffed right now. Not "could use another person someday" — actively short a paralegal, or running people at 110%.

**[Wait for hands. There will be many. Acknowledge it.]**

> Yeah. That's most rooms I do this in.
>
> So here's what's true: AI saves you time on individual tasks. It's real, and you should use it. But it doesn't reduce the volume of work coming in the door. Your firm doesn't have an efficiency problem. It has a capacity problem. Those are different things, and they need different solutions.

---

## PART 2: Introducing the Solution (5 min)

### [Slide: "When AI Isn't Enough"]

**Script:**

> Everything you learned today is the efficiency piece. Prompting, workflows, agents — that's how you work smarter with the people you have.
>
> But for firms that are genuinely understaffed — and most of you just told me you are — there's a second piece. And that's what I spend most of my time on outside of these workshops.

**[Transition — conversational, not salesy.]**

> I run a company called VerdictOps. What we do is simple: remote paralegals, already trained on AI tools, who plug into your existing workflow. Not a temp agency. Not a technology product. An operations solution.
>
> Your discovery pile is backed up? We put a paralegal on it who already knows how to use the tools you just learned today. Your associate is drowning in document review? We handle the first pass so they can focus on analysis.
>
> It's not for every firm. If you're a solo practitioner doing great, you don't need it. If your only problem is that your team needs better prompts — that's what today was for, and you're set.
>
> But if your problem is that there's more work than people — AI prompting alone won't fix that. You need capacity AND efficiency. That's where VerdictOps fits.

---

## PART 3: The Offer + CTA (5 min)

### [Slide: "Free AI Readiness Assessment" — with QR code to booking page]

**Script:**

> So here's what I want to offer everyone in this room.
>
> It's called an AI Readiness Assessment. It's free. It's 30 minutes. And it's not a sales call — I need to be clear about that because I hate when workshops turn into infomercials.
>
> Here's what it actually is: you tell me what's backed up at your firm. What's taking too long. Where people are stretched. And I'll tell you one of three things:

**[Count on fingers.]**

> One — you just need better AI habits. Today gave you that. Go execute, you're good.
>
> Two — you have a capacity problem that AI won't solve. VerdictOps might be right for your firm, and I'll explain how it works.
>
> Three — you need both. Start with AI training for your team, add VerdictOps for the overflow. Most firms are actually here.
>
> Either way, you walk away with a clear picture of where your firm stands. No pitch. Just a diagnosis.

**[Point to the back of the room.]**

> There's a sign-up sheet at the back table. Write your name and email, and my team will send you a calendar link tomorrow to book your 30 minutes.
>
> Or — if you're the type who does things now — there's a QR code on this slide. Scan it, pick a time, done.

**[Leave the slide up.]**

> You'll also get an email from me tonight with everything from today — the templates, the quick-reference card, your CounterbenchAI trial access, and the Assessment booking link. So you don't have to decide right now.
>
> But if you've been sitting here thinking "this is great, but my firm's real problem is that we're three people short" — the Assessment is built for you. I'd love to talk.

## DELIVERY NOTES

### Room Setup
- **Back table**: Sign-up sheet with columns for Name, Email, Firm Name, Phone (optional). Have pens ready.
- **Slide**: QR code must link to the Assessment booking page (Calendly or equivalent). Test before the workshop.
- **Handout insert**: Include an Assessment one-pager in the materials packet — attendees who don't sign up today will find it later.

### Timing Cues
- Part 1 (Reality Check): 5 min. Don't rush the hand-raise moment — that's the emotional anchor.
- Part 2 (VerdictOps intro): 5 min. Keep it conversational. If someone asks a question, answer it — this is where trust solidifies.
- Part 3 (Offer + CTA): 5 min. End clean. Don't oversell. The line "No pitch. Just a diagnosis." must land with conviction.

### What NOT To Do
- Don't mention VerdictOps pricing from the stage. That's for the Assessment call.
- Don't make this feel like a bait-and-switch. The workshop delivered 7 hours of real value before this section. Lean on that.
- Don't pressure. "You don't have to decide right now" is genuine — the follow-up email sequence does the work.
- Don't skip the "it's not for every firm" qualifier. That's what makes it credible. Disqualifying some people makes the offer real for everyone else.

### Qualification During the Section
While delivering Part 1, note who raises their hand enthusiastically during the understaffing question. Cross-reference with registration data (role, firm size). After the session ends, prioritize conversations with:
- **Office Managers / Partners at 6-50 attorney firms** who raised their hand = VerdictOps pipeline (high priority)
- **Paralegals who said "my boss sent me"** = get the managing partner's name for the follow-up sequence (medium priority)
- **Solo practitioners** = CounterbenchAI product leads, not VerdictOps (redirect to product trial)

### Post-Section Flow
1. After the CTA, transition to final workshop close: "Thank you all for being here today" etc.
2. Mingle. Walk the room. Be at the back table when people approach it.
3. Collect sign-up sheets before leaving. Photograph as backup.
4. Same evening: trigger the post-workshop email sequence (Track A for paralegals, Track B for decision-makers — per PRD-workshop-verdictops-pipeline.md).
